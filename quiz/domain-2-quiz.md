> 15 practice questions. Answers at the bottom.

## Questions

## Answers

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **B** | Always start with a Stakeholder Map — you need to know who matters before producing anything else |
| 2 | **B** | High influence + negative attitude = Opponents. Action = Satisfy (not fight, not ignore) |
| 3 | **C** | BMC sequence: Value Proposition → Customer Segments → **Channels** → Customer Relationships |
| 4 | **C** | SAP EA maps TOGAF "Driver" to "Strategic Priority" |
| 5 | **B** | 10–20 principles maximum. More = less flexibility. Reduce the set. |
| 6 | **C** | Statement of Architecture Work = output of Phase A, approved before Phase B begins |
| 7 | **B** | 10-minute rule: if a stakeholder can't understand in 10 minutes, the diagram is too complex. Simplify. |
| 8 | **C** | Rationale = the business benefits of adhering to the principle |
| 9 | **C** | One entity type per roadmap — don't mix Initiatives, Capabilities, and Outcomes |
| 10 | **B** | Context = org relationships. Concept = technical building blocks (pencil sketch). |
| 11 | **C** | Strategy Map: Strategic Priority → Goals → Value Drivers → **Business Capabilities** → Initiatives |
| 12 | **C** | High tech fit + low functional fit = **Tolerate** |
| 13 | **B** | Transition Architecture = formally defined intermediate state on journey to Target |
| 14 | **C** | CIO is critical — they bridge business and IT at executive level |
| 15 | **C** | Outcome-based roadmaps resonate best with business stakeholders |
