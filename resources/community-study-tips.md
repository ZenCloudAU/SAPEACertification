# Community Study Tips — From Certified SAP EA Practitioners

> Curated from SAP Community blogs by certified practitioners. These are real-world lessons, not theory.

---

## Key Insight: TOGAF Is Not Enough

> *"I initially approached the SAP EA certification with a TOGAF enterprise architecture mindset. While TOGAF provides a strong and valuable foundation, it is not sufficient on its own."*
> — JansiMurugesan, SAP Community (Dec 2025)

This is the most important warning from the community. If you come from a TOGAF background, you will be tempted to answer questions using TOGAF logic. The exam requires:

- Deep understanding of **SAP EAF specifically** — not generic EA frameworks
- **SAP-specific terminology** — Strategic Priority ≠ Driver, Value Driver ≠ Objective
- **SAP Reference Architecture** content — RBA, RSA, and how they connect
- How SAP **positions EA within real customer scenarios** — not abstract theory

---

## What the Exam Actually Tests

The exam is scenario-heavy. You need to answer:

1. **What is the business problem?**
2. **What solution fits best?**
3. **Why are the other options wrong?**

Common question types:
- Choosing the right architecture for a business need
- Identifying the best integration or deployment options
- Evaluating different solutions and making architectural decisions

> *"Focus on the 'why,' not just the 'what': Understand why certain architectures or strategies are chosen for specific business needs."*

## The Single Most Important Study Tip

> *"Don't skip visuals! Diagrams and images in the learning journeys are crucial for understanding how SAP components interact."*

This was emphasised by both the community blog author and the Learning Hub coach. The Metro Map, the four-view matrix, the EA Alignment Model, the RBA/RSA structure — these are not decorative. They are testable content.

**What to do with every diagram:**
- Understand what each element represents
- Understand the relationships between elements
- Understand why the diagram is structured that way
- Be able to reconstruct it from memory

## Exam Attempt Reality Check

The community is honest: **many people do not pass on the first attempt.**

> *"No, I didn't pass on my first try. It took me a few attempts."*

What changed after failing:
- Reviewed score reports to identify weak domains
- Changed approach from TOGAF-first to SAP EAF-first thinking
- Deep-dived into diagrams and images (previously skipped)
- Focused on scenario-based reasoning, not memorisation

**If you fail:** Use the score report. It tells you which domains need work. Go back to the specific learning journey content for those domains.

## Time and Mindset

> *"The goal isn't to race through the material — it's about truly understanding the concepts. I treated my preparation as a journey, not just an exam."*

- Focus on **understanding over speed**
- Apply concepts to **real customer scenarios** as you learn
- Consistency beats cramming
- The 80% pass mark means no weak areas — every domain matters equally

## Who Should Pursue This Certification

The certification is relevant for:
- Those working with SAP solutions or IT architecture
- Those aiming to become an Enterprise or SAP Solution Architect
- Professionals in leadership roles focused on business transformation or IT strategy
- Anyone wanting to understand SAP's entire ecosystem — not just isolated tools

> *"Think beyond certification — use SAP EA as a tool to drive real-world impact."*
