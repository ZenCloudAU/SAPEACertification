## The Three C-Suite Perspectives EA Must Serve

Before trends — the foundational framing from Peter Klee:

| Executive | Focus | EA Northstar |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| **CEO** | Evolving the business, adopting innovation | Business transformation, M&A, capability gap closure |
| **CFO** | Reducing complexity, optimising cost | Landscape rationalisation, TCO reduction, public cloud |
| **CIO** | Managing complexity, enabling future state | Simplification, clean architecture, landscape consolidation |

> EA must derive a northstar architecture that serves all three perspectives simultaneously. This is why EA sits at the intersection of business and IT — not inside either.

## Five EA Trends

### Trend 1 — AI-Supported Enterprise Architecture ⭐ HIGH RELEVANCE

**Likelihood:** Very high. Already happening.
**Impact:** High.

Two dimensions:

**AI for EA (AI as EA coworker):**
- Insights into landscape dependencies
- Pattern recognition for optimisation opportunities
- Decision support for cost, risk, and tradeoff analysis
- Efficiency acceleration: business capability mapping, strategy mapping
- Already in LeanIX today — growing rapidly

**EA for AI (EA supporting AI initiatives):**
- Align AI initiatives with overall architecture planning
- Data architecture design for AI (data quality, governance, lineage)
- Compliance and security framework for AI deployment
- Capability mapping to identify AI-enablement opportunities per process

**SAP's AI principles (Relevant, Reliable, Responsible):**
- Relevant — business AI that addresses real problems
- Reliable — tested, validated, consistent
- Responsible — ethical, high security and privacy standards, compliant

> **Career relevance:** The Brisbane role mentions AI integration. Knowing how to position AI within an architecture capability map (capability → process → AI scenario) is a differentiating skill.

---

### Trend 2 — Continuous Clean Architecture ⭐ HIGH RELEVANCE

**Likelihood:** High. Evolving from Clean Core.
**Impact:** High.

The evolution from Clean Core (S/4HANA-specific) to Clean Architecture (enterprise-wide):

```
Define Architectural KPIs
        ↓
Assess current state against KPIs
        ↓
Plan target architecture and roadmap
        ↓
Implement
        ↓
Measure continuously → adjust → repeat
```

**Why this matters:**
- Gives EA a measurable value proposition — not just diagrams
- Provides governance through continuous measurement rather than one-off assessments
- Directly addresses the CFO's complexity reduction agenda
- LeanIX dashboarding capabilities already support this

> **Career relevance:** The Brisbane role asks to "reduce application sprawl and complexity over time." This is the governance model for doing that continuously. Define KPIs, measure, adjust — not a project, a practice.

### Trend 3 — EA-Driven Innovation / EA as Digital Twin

**Likelihood:** Medium-high. Directionally clear.
**Impact:** High when achieved.

**Current state:** Architecture is updated periodically — monthly or quarterly effort to collect and update data.

**Future state:** Live architecture with auto-discovery. Changes in the physical landscape automatically reflected in the EA model.

Further evolution: **Simulation capabilities** — model a proposed change and see how the architecture would respond before implementing.

**LeanIX direction:** Integration with Cloud ALM already provides near-real-time landscape data. Auto-discovery from BTP and Cloud ALM environments is available today.

### Trend 4 — Agile and Iterative Architecture

**Likelihood:** High. Already happening.
**Impact:** Medium-high.

Moving away from:
- Big-bang EA programmes
- Waterfall EA methodology
- Pre-project EA that disappears after delivery

Moving toward:
- Continuous iterative EA engagement
- EA embedded in transformation programmes
- Agile ways of working applied to architecture

> *"Enterprise architecture needs to become much more tangible, integrated in real transformation projects — not seen as a pre-project and then you're off."* — Peter Klee

**SAP EAF already supports this:** The Metro Map is explicitly non-waterfall. The ADM is iterative at four levels. This trend is about changing the culture of how EA is practised, not changing the framework.

---

### Trend 5 — Quantum Computing (Forward-Looking)

**Likelihood:** Low-medium within 3-4 years at industry scale. High long-term.
**Impact:** Transformational when it arrives.

**Areas where quantum computing will impact architecture:**
- Inventory optimisation
- Demand forecasting
- Production planning (huge for manufacturing)
- Supply chain optimisation

**Architectural implications when it arrives:**
- Data architecture changes — qubits work differently from classical data storage
- Instance strategy — quantum computers cannot be combined with classical server infrastructure
- Scaling strategy — different model
- AI and quantum computing integration
- Integration with existing SAP landscape

> Not imminent at scale. But large companies including SAP are actively investing. Enterprise architects will be at the front when it arrives.

## Framework Interrelation — The Key Insight from the Webcast

Peter Klee's most important statement:

> *"The dimensions of methodology, reference content, and tooling are very much interrelated. The reference content is developed following the methodology and is available in our tooling. When SAP Product Engineering designs new products, they document business capabilities, business processes, and technologies according to the methodology and make it available in the tooling."*

**Practical implication:** The RBA/RSA is not a static document — it is continuously updated by SAP product teams using the same methodology. When you access the Business Accelerator Hub, you are seeing the current state of how SAP positions its products against the business architecture.

## The 3D-ADM — Three Domains Where SAP Uses Its Own ADM

| Domain | Usage |
|--------|-------|
| **Product Engineering** | SAP architects use the EA methodology to design and document new products |
