Domain 1: SAP EA Framework & Toolset

20–30% of exam · ~8–12 questions
The 5 Building Blocks ▼
⚠️ Memorise these cold. Every Domain 1 question sits inside one of these five.
Building BlockWhat It IsExam Signal Words
MethodologyTOGAF v10-based, tailored for SAP. Reduces tailoring effort per engagement."ADM", "phases", "iterative", "how to do EA"
Reference Architecture ContentPre-built RBA + RSA blueprints. APQC-based. Adapt rather than invent."pre-built", "reference", "RBA", "RSA", "accelerate"
ToolingLeanIX + Signavio + Cloud ALM + Business Accelerator Hub"tools", "LeanIX", "Signavio", "Cloud ALM"
PracticeOrganisational implementation of EA. Governance, operating model, maturity."org model", "governance", "maturity", "team"
ServicesNorth Star Architecture. SAP-delivered engagement services."North Star", "engagement", "delivery", "modules"
⚠️ Trap: Practice = internal org model. Services = external SAP engagement model. They sound similar — they are different.
ADM Phases ▼
Preliminary → A: Architecture Vision → B: Business Architecture → C: Application & Data → D: Technology → E: Opportunities & Solutions
Running through ALL phases: Requirements Management · Governance (Risk, Decisions, Principles)
⚠️ Critical: NOT a waterfall. Iterations happen within phases, between phases, and between full cycles.

3 Types of Tailoring (Preliminary Phase)

TypeWhat It Covers
TerminologyAgreed glossary. Business-understood terms. Enterprise Glossary document.
ContentAdopt third-party frameworks, customise classification.
ProcessRemove duplicate tasks, add org-specific checkpoints, align with portfolio mgmt.
The Metro Map ▼

SAP's visual representation of the entire methodology — like a tube/subway map. Shows the full set of artifacts, inputs from SAP Reference Architecture, and references to existing work products.

3 Variants

VariantWhen to Use
Business-CentricEngagement driven by business transformation. Fewer IT artifacts.
IT-CentricEngagement driven by technology change. Lighter business artifacts.
HolisticFull end-to-end. All domains from strategy through technology.
Artifact selection is always driven by stakeholder needs, not architectural completeness.
The Integrated Toolchain ▼
StepToolsWhat Happens
1. INGESTLeanIX ↔ Cloud ALMImport current SAP system landscape
2. ANALYSESignavioProcess performance. 1,000+ built-in metrics. 24hr connection.
3. DESIGNLeanIX + SignavioTarget architecture + future process models.
4. PLANLeanIX → Cloud ALMRoadmaps handed over. Implementation projects generated.
5. EXECUTESignavio + Cloud ALMProcess designs transferred. Operations + monitoring.

Tool Roles

ToolPrimary Role
SAP LeanIXArchitecture Management. Application Portfolio, Roadmaps, Technology Risk.
SAP SignavioProcess Management. Process Insights (performance), Process Intelligence (conformance), Process Explorer (RBA content).
SAP Cloud ALMProject Execution + Operations. Receives roadmaps from LeanIX.
Business Accelerator HubAPIs, iFlows, Solution Process references. api.sap.com
⚠️ LeanIX = architecture/applications. Signavio = processes. Don't swap them.

SeparatedLoosely coupled divisions, different architecturesLegacy of acquisitions. Synergies missed.

EA Practice Maturity — 8 Dimensions ▼
DimensionWhat It Measures
Business-IT AlignmentTraceability between business objectives and IT capabilities
Stakeholder InvolvementIdentification, engagement, and awareness of EA initiatives
Action & ImpactWhether EA actually influences decisions on sourcing, investment, strategy
Architecture DevelopmentComprehensiveness and standardisation of deliverables

5 Maturity Levels: Ad-Hoc → Initial → Defined → Managed → Optimised · Output: spider web diagram
Risk & Decision Management ▼
Initial Risk = before mitigation · Residual Risk = after mitigation actions applied
⚠️ Architecture produced AFTER decisions = too late and causes conflict. EAs must be involved BEFORE key decisions are made.

Risk domains: Business · Data · Application · Technology
Process: Identify → Assess (probability + impact) → Mitigate → Re-assess residual

Domain 2: Architecture Vision & Roadmap

20–30% of exam · ~8–12 questions
Strategy → Model → Architecture Hierarchy ▼
LayerDefinitionExample
Business StrategyThe goals the enterprise wants to achieve + metrics"Increase market share by 20% in 3 years"
Business ModelHOW the enterprise seeks to profit — value proposition, competitive approach"Subscription-based SaaS with freemium entry"
Business ArchitectureThe structure — capabilities, processes, information, organisation — to execute the model"We need a Customer Management capability at maturity level 3"
⚠️ These are hierarchical: Strategy → Model → Architecture. Do not confuse them.
Stakeholder Map — 4 Quadrants ▼
QuadrantInfluenceAttitudeAction
PromotersHighPositiveActively engage — use them to influence others
EnthusiastsLowPositiveInform — supportive but limited power
OpponentsHighNegativeSatisfy — highest priority, can kill the project
ResistersLowNegativeMonitor and respond — low risk but don't ignore
⚠️ Opponents are MORE dangerous than Resisters (both negative) because Opponents have HIGH influence — they can actually stop the engagement. Action = Satisfy, not fight.
Business Model Canvas — 9 Blocks in Order ⭐ ▼
⚠️ The exam specifically tests the SEQUENCE. Memorise this order.
#BlockQuestion It Answers
1Value PropositionWhat value do we deliver? What problems do we solve?
2Customer SegmentsFor whom? Who are our most important customers?
3ChannelsHow do we reach them? How do we deliver the value?
4Customer RelationshipsWhat type of relationship does each segment expect?
5Revenue StreamsWhat are customers willing to pay for?
6Key ResourcesWhat assets does our value proposition require?
7Key ActivitiesWhat activities does our value proposition require?
8Key PartnersWho are our key suppliers/partners? What do we outsource?
9Cost StructureWhat are the most important costs in our business model?
Blocks 1–5: External / value delivery · Blocks 6–8: Internal / value creation · Block 9: Financial reality (always last)
Architecture Principles ▼
ComponentDescription
NameShort, memorable, technology-neutral. No ambiguous words: "support", "open", "consider", "avoid".
StatementSuccinctly and unambiguously communicates the fundamental rule.
RationaleBusiness benefits of adhering. Business language.
ImplicationsRequirements (costs, activities, resources) for both business and IT.
Maximum 10–20 principles. Too many = limits architectural flexibility.
Created through a consensus workshop — consensus means everyone can live with it, not 100% agreement.

Domain 3: Business Architecture

20–30% of exam · ~8–12 questions
Two Main Approaches ▼
Business Architecture is a prerequisite for all other domains. It must be completed first.
ApproachFocusAnchor PointTools
Capability-CentricWHAT the business doesBusiness Capabilities → Solution Capabilities → ApplicationsSAP LeanIX, SAP Signavio
Process-CentricHOW the business does itBusiness Process Model → Solution Process Model → ApplicationsSAP Signavio Process Explorer
Business Capability Map ▼
Capabilities define WHAT the business does — independent of how technology supports it.

3-Level Hierarchy

LevelNameDescription
Level 1Business DomainHighest grouping. Four enterprise domains.
Level 2Business AreaGroups of related capabilities within a domain.
Level 3Business CapabilitySpecific ability the org must possess.

4 Enterprise Domains (Level 1)

1. Product & Services · 2. Customer · 3. Supply · 4. Corporate

MECE Principle

  • Mutually Exclusive — no overlap between capabilities
  • Collectively Exhaustive — no gaps in coverage
⚠️ Business stakeholders OWN and NAME capabilities. Naming must resonate with business language, not IT jargon.
Business Process Model — 4 Levels ▼
LevelNameNaming Convention
Level 1E2E Business ProcessStarts "Plan to Optimise…", ends "Manage…" (PDCA cycle)
Level 2Business Process ModuleFormat: <A to B> e.g. "Hire to Retire". "Manage…" = pervasive
Level 3Business Process SegmentNamed same as related Business Area in BCM (creates synergy)
Level 4Business ActivityLowest value-creating step. ONE central Business Activity Repository.
Based on APQC Process Classification Framework (PCF) v7.2.1
⚠️ Level 3 Business Process Segment shares its name with the Business Area in the BCM — intentional! They are different objects with the same name. This creates synergy, not an error.
Key Business Architecture Artifacts ▼
ArtifactWhat It ShowsKey Rule
Organisation MapNetwork of relationships between org units, partners, stakeholder groupsNOT an org chart — shows working relationships, not reporting hierarchy
Business Capability MapWhat the org must be able to do. 3 levels. MECE.Business owns it
Business Value Flow DiagramValue-adding activities from a value perspectiveStarting point for process decomposition
Business Data CatalogBusiness-relevant information objects and their relationshipsConnected to capabilities and processes. Source: SAP One Domain Model.
Business Footprint Diagram ⭐The "X-ray" — links org units → strategic goals → processes → capabilities → solution components → technologyCross-domain artifact. Links ALL architecture domains in one view.

Gap Analysis: Baseline Architecture (as-is) → Target Architecture (to-be) → Gaps → Roadmap (Phase E)

LensAssessesOutput

Technology Capability AssessmentFeasibility of current IT landscapeTechnology Capability Heatmap

Domain 4: Data, Application & Technology Architecture

20–30% of exam · ~8–12 questions
6 Application Architecture Diagrams ▼
DiagramWhat It ShowsKey Use
Product MapRecommended products/solution components per Business Domain or Area"What SAP products do we need?" — Bill of Materials
Solution Component DiagramBlueprint of solution components and communication channels (STRUCTURE)"How do the systems connect?" — static view
Solution Value Flow DiagramHigh-level process — value-adding activities mapped to solution components"What value does each system deliver?"
Solution Process Flow DiagramDetailed BPMN 2.0 collaboration diagram (BEHAVIOUR)"What exactly happens step by step?" — dynamic view

Software Distribution DiagramHow solution components are distributed across infrastructure"Where does everything live?" Phase C → feeds Phase D E&L diagram
⚠️ Solution Component Diagram = STRUCTURE (static). Solution Process Flow = BEHAVIOUR (dynamic). Most commonly confused pair.
Deployment Types & Cloud Service Models ▼

Deployment Types

TypeDefinition
On-PremiseSoftware installed and runs on the organisation's own computers/data centres.
Private CloudCloud for exclusive use of a single organisation (single-tenant). Accessed via VPN. Managed by org or third party.
Public CloudCloud for open use by the general public (multi-tenant). Managed by cloud provider.
⚠️ Private Cloud ≠ On-Premise. Private Cloud is still a cloud deployment — just single-tenant and accessed via VPN.

Cloud Service Models

ModelProvider ManagesCustomer Controls
SaaSEverything including the applicationUser-specific config only. No control over releases.
PaaSInfrastructure + platform/middlewareDeployed applications + platform configuration
IaaSPhysical hardware onlyOS, storage, deployed applications, selected network (VLAN)
IaaS = most customer control. SaaS = least customer control (user config only).
SAP Clean Core — 3 Extension Types ⭐ ▼
Clean Core = keeping S/4HANA standard and unmodified. One of the most exam-tested topics.
Extension TypeWhere It RunsHow It Extends
Key User ExtensibilityOn S/4HANA itselfBusiness users configure without code. In-app tools. Low risk.
Developer ExtensibilityOn S/4HANA itself (ABAP Environment)Developers extend using SAP's stable, published APIs. No modification of SAP code.
Side-by-Side ExtensionsOn SAP BTPSeparate system entirely. Connected via APIs. Maximum flexibility and isolation.
New complex extensions → Side-by-Side on BTP. Simple user config → Key User. ABAP dev on S/4HANA → Developer Extensibility.
ISA-M — 4 Integration Domains ▼
DomainWhat It Covers
Process IntegrationConnecting business processes across systems (A2A and B2B)
Data IntegrationMoving and synchronising data between systems
Analytics IntegrationConnecting operational data to analytics and reporting systems
IoT IntegrationConnecting devices and things to enterprise systems

Integration Styles

Synchronous (request/reply) · Asynchronous (event-driven) · Batch (scheduled) · Real-time (immediate)

StrategyDescriptionWhen to Use

Technology Architecture (Phase D) ▼

Describes the technology stack, physical computing environment, and network connectivity that enable the Application and Data Architecture building blocks.

Environments & Location Diagram

Evolves from the Software Distribution Diagram (Phase C) into a more detailed deployment view. Names data centre providers, locations, infrastructure, and maps building blocks to environments.

⚠️ Software Distribution Diagram = Phase C (Application Architecture). Environments & Location Diagram = Phase D (Technology Architecture). The SD is the INPUT to the E&L diagram.

Exam Traps

31 common mistakes that catch candidates out. Review the night before exam day.

Domain 1

⚠️ Practice ≠ Services

Practice = the internal organisational model of EA within a company. Services = the external SAP engagement offering (North Star Architecture). They sound similar — completely different things.

⚠️ ADM is NOT waterfall

The ADM is iterative — within phases, between phases, between full cycles. Never describe it as a sequential waterfall process.

⚠️ Artifacts are for stakeholders, not architecture

When asked why you choose a subset of Metro Map artifacts, the answer is always stakeholder needs — not "to cover all architecture domains" or "for completeness."

⚠️ LeanIX vs Signavio

LeanIX = architecture and applications management. Signavio = business process management and analysis. Don't swap them.

⚠️ Maturity model has 8 dimensions — all testable

Don't confuse the 8 maturity dimensions with the 5 building blocks or the ADM phases.

Domain 2

⚠️ BMC sequence is specifically tested

Order: Value Proposition → Customer Segments → Channels → Customer Relationships → Revenue Streams → Key Resources → Key Activities → Key Partners → Cost Structure. External (1–5) before internal (6–8). Cost (9) is always last.

⚠️ Strategy vs Model vs Architecture

Strategy = goals & metrics. Model = how you profit. Architecture = structure to execute the model. Hierarchical — don't conflate them.

⚠️ Statement of Architecture Work timing

Must be signed and approved at the end of Phase A. Phase B cannot begin without it. Architecture produced after a decision = too late.

⚠️ Architecture Principles — 10–20 maximum

More than 20 principles limits architectural flexibility. If a scenario describes 40 principles, that is a problem, not a best practice.

⚠️ Roadmap entity types — don't mix

Business roadmaps can show: Initiatives OR Capabilities OR Outcomes. Pick one. Mixing entity types is incorrect. Outcome-based resonates most with business stakeholders.

⚠️ Solution Context vs Solution Concept

Solution Context = organisational relationships. Solution Concept = technical building blocks (pencil sketch). Both are Phase A artifacts but serve different purposes.

⚠️ Opponents vs Resisters

Both have negative attitude. Opponents = High influence → Satisfy (highest priority). Resisters = Low influence → Monitor and respond. Don't swap the actions.

⚠️ TOGAF → SAP EA term changes

TOGAF "Driver" → SAP EA "Strategic Priority". TOGAF "Objective" → SAP EA "Value Driver".

Domain 3

⚠️ Capability vs Process

Capability = WHAT the business does (stable, rarely changes, independent of technology). Process = HOW the business does it (continually improved).

⚠️ Business owns capabilities

Business people name and own the capabilities. Naming must resonate with business language, not IT jargon.

⚠️ MECE — both conditions required simultaneously

Mutually Exclusive AND Collectively Exhaustive — both must be true at the same time. Not just one or the other.

⚠️ Business Footprint Diagram is cross-domain

It is NOT just a business architecture diagram. It links strategy → capabilities → solution components → technology. It crosses ALL architecture domains.

⚠️ Level 3 Business Process Segment naming

Named the same as the corresponding Business Area in the BCM. This is intentional — it creates synergy. They are different objects that happen to share the same name.

⚠️ Organisation Map ≠ Org Chart

Org Map = network of working relationships. Org Chart = hierarchical reporting structure. EA uses Org Maps.

⚠️ Business Activity Repository

ALL Level 4 Business Activities are stored in ONE central repository. Names can change per industry but the inherent meaning cannot.

Domain 4

⚠️ Solution Component vs Solution Process Flow diagrams

Solution Component Diagram = STRUCTURE (static view). Solution Process Flow = BEHAVIOUR (dynamic step-by-step flow). Most commonly confused pair in this domain.

⚠️ Private Cloud ≠ On-Premise

Private Cloud is still a cloud deployment — single-tenant, can be managed by org or third party. On-Premise = physically on the organisation's own premises.

⚠️ SaaS customer control

In SaaS: customer controls ONLY user-specific configuration. No control over releases, underlying infrastructure, or platform. IaaS gives the MOST customer control.

⚠️ Clean Core extension location

Key User = ON S/4HANA (no code). Developer = ON S/4HANA (ABAP, stable APIs). Side-by-Side = ON BTP (separate system, APIs). Don't confuse where each runs.

⚠️ Software Distribution Diagram phase

Belongs to Phase C (Application Architecture). Provides INPUT to the Environments & Location Diagram in Phase D. They are different diagrams at different levels of detail.

⚠️ ISA-M has 4 domains

Process Integration · Data Integration · Analytics Integration · IoT Integration. All four are individually testable.

General

⚠️ Wanderlust = 45% of the exam

18/40 questions = 45% of the exam come from the Wanderlust case study. It is published in advance. Read it BEFORE exam day.

⚠️ Transition Architecture vs Target Architecture

Target Architecture = final desired state. Transition Architecture = formally defined intermediate state on the journey to the target. Multiple Transition Architectures may exist.

⚠️ Requirements Management is cross-cutting

Requirements Management runs through ALL ADM phases. It is NOT confined to Preliminary or Phase A. Same applies to Governance (Risk, Decisions, Principles).

⚠️ Best answer, not only correct answer

SAP exam questions look for the MOST appropriate response in context. Read all four options before selecting. An answer that's right in one scenario may be wrong in another.
